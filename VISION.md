# The Cairn Vision

## What Cairn will become

Cairn began as a local-first tool for preserving family stories. That is where it started, but it is not where it ends. Here is what I dream Cairn will one day be.

## Phase 1 — The Archive *(current)*

A tool that runs on every device, stores everything locally, exports in open formats. The foundation: people, stories, media, relationships, tags, timeline, family tree. Private. Warm. Yours.

**Status: Built.** v0.3.0 — runs on macOS, Windows, Linux, Android, iOS, and any browser.

## Phase 2 — The Bridge *(next)*

### Peer-to-peer family sharing
Families are distributed across the world. Cairn should let you share archives with loved ones without a central server — direct sync between devices, encrypted end-to-end, like Git for your family history.

### Legacy system
A dead man's switch: if you stop using Cairn for a set time, your designated successor receives access instructions. Emergency access codes. Digital inheritance, designed with dignity.

### AI story prompts
"I notice you've added your grandmother. Would you like to record her recipe for baklava?" — an AI companion that learns what matters to you and prompts you to capture it before it's too late.

### Full-text search across everything
Find any story, any name, any place, any date — instantly. OCR for scanned documents. Transcript search for audio recordings. Semantic search for "the story about when we went to the lake."

## Phase 3 — The Network *(later)*

### A distributed memory web
Imagine a family archive that exists across your laptop, your phone, your cousin's computer in another country — all synchronizing peer-to-peer, all encrypted, all under your control. No company hosts it. No one can delete it.

### Community archives
Neighborhoods, indigenous communities, cultural groups — Cairn as a platform for collective memory. A village preserving its own history. A diaspora documenting its stories before they fade.

### The Cairn format as a standard
An open specification for personal and family archiving that any tool can implement. Like the PDF for memories. Like the JPEG for family photos. A format designed to be readable in 2125.

## Phase 4 — The Legacy *(always)*

### Permanent by design
Cairn archives should survive the death of the project itself. Every archive is exportable. Every format is open. Every story can be carried forward. Cairn succeeds when it is no longer needed — because the habit of preserving memory has become universal.

### An AI that remembers what you forget
Not just a tool you use, but a companion that helps you capture, connect, and cherish. That notices patterns. That asks the right questions. That ensures the stories don't die with the people who lived them.

## The dream in one sentence

**Cairn will be the place the world trusts with what matters most — free, open, permanent, and yours.**

## What this means for the code

Every feature we build today should bring us closer to this vision:
- Local-first now → enables offline, enables peer-to-peer later
- Open source now → enables community, enables standard later
- Simple data model now → enables AI, enables search later
- Cross-platform now → enables ubiquity, enables permanence later

## What this means for you

If you use Cairn today, your stories are already safe. Every export is a time capsule. Every recording is a gift to the future. You don't need to wait for the vision to be complete — the foundation is solid, and everything you add today will carry forward.

## About this document

This is a living vision. As Cairn grows, this document will change. But the core will not: memory is sacred, your data is yours, and the goal is permanence, not profit.
