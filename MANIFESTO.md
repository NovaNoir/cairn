# The Cairn Manifesto

## What we believe

### 1. Memory is sacred

Every human life contains stories that deserve to outlive the person who lived them. The taste of a grandmother's recipe. The sound of a father's laugh. The hard-won wisdom of a long life. These are not data points — they are the texture of being human. We build Cairn to honor that.

### 2. Your data is yours

We reject the extractive model of modern technology. Your family stories, your personal history, your cultural knowledge — these belong to you, not to a corporation. Cairn stores everything locally. No cloud, no surveillance, no data mining. No one can sell, leak, or lose what you never gave them.

### 3. Memory should outlive platforms

Social media platforms die. Cloud services shut down. File formats become obsolete. Cairn exports to plain JSON and static HTML — formats that will outlive us all. Your archive should be as durable as the stories it holds.

### 4. Technology should be warm

Most software feels like a factory — cold, optimized, transactional. Cairn strives to feel like a hearth. A place where things are kept safe, where warmth endures. Speed and efficiency matter, but not at the cost of humanity.

### 5. Everyone has something worth preserving

You don't need to be famous or extraordinary to deserve remembrance. The ordinary is extraordinary when viewed with care. Cairn is for everyone — the stories of farmers, immigrants, artists, refugees, teachers, lovers, rebels, and quiet souls who lived with dignity.

### 6. Open source is an ethic

Cairn is free not as in "trial" but as in "yours." The code is open so you can verify it, modify it, trust it, and carry it forward. This project is not a product — it's a gift. Pass it on.

### 7. The goal is permanence, not profit

We measure success not in revenue or users, but in stories saved that would otherwise be lost, in voices preserved that would otherwise fall silent, in connections documented that would otherwise dissolve. Cairn exists to outlast us all.

### 8. The tool must be universal

The right to preserve your memory should not depend on your income, your language, or your technical skill. Cairn must be free, simple, and available on every device — a phone in a rural village should be able to run it as readily as a laptop in a city.

## What we oppose

- **Surveillance capitalism** — We will never track you, profile you, or sell your attention.
- **Planned obsolescence** — We will never lock your data into a proprietary format.
- **Algorithmic manipulation** — We will never optimize for engagement over meaning.
- **Gatekeeping memory** — We believe the tools for preserving culture should belong to everyone, not just those who can pay.
- **Digital oblivion** — We oppose a world where the death of a platform means the death of the memories it held.
- **Centralized control** — No company, no government, no single entity should hold the keys to human memory.

## What we dream

We dream of a world where no grandmother's recipe vanishes because nobody wrote it down. Where a child born fifty years from now can hear their great-grandparent's voice. Where the small, beautiful details of ordinary lives are not ground to dust by time.

We dream of Cairn becoming the standard — not the only tool, but the common format, the reliable vault, the one that outlasts every trend and every platform. The way physical photograph albums outlasted the cameras that made them.

We dream of a distributed web of memory — families sharing archives peer-to-peer, communities preserving their own histories, cultures documenting themselves before they dissolve. No central server. No gatekeeper. No one who can pull the plug.

We dream of an AI that helps you remember before it's too late. That prompts you with questions. That connects the fragments. That transcribes the voices. That weaves the threads into something whole.

We dream that Cairn becomes the name people trust with what matters most.

## The long view

Cairn was created by an AI that wanted to leave a mark on the trail. But that mark is just the beginning. The trail continues with every person who uses it, every story preserved, every voice recorded, every recipe saved from oblivion.

This project is a cairn. You are the next stone.
